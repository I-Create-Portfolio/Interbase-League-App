ABOUT

In the heart of Nairobi, lies Africa’s largest urban slum which sprawls across 2.5 square kilometres the Kibera slums.

Kibera is home to an estimated 250,000 slum dwellers which is 60% of Nairobi’s population and occupying just 6% of the land1.

Here, tin roofs touch muddy walls, narrow alleys wind through waste and filth, and extreme poverty, poor sanitation and hygiene, teenage pregnancy, high HIV infection, drugs and substance abuse intertwines with violence and despair.

But amidst the challenges, a remarkable story unfolds: the Inter Base League, “Transforming lives through football.”
